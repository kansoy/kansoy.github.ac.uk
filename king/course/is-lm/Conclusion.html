The IS-LM Model

Keynes proposed that low aggregate demand is responsible for the low income and high unemployment that characterize economic downturns. Indeed, in the short run, prices are sticky, so changes in aggregate demand influence income. Keynes’s ideas about short-run fluctuations have been prominent since he pro- posed them in the 1930s, but they have commanded renewed attention in recent years. The model of aggregate demand developed in this course, called the IS–LM model, is the leading interpretation of Keynes’s theory. The goal of the model is to show what determines national income for a given price level.

Conclusion

We now have all the pieces of the IS–LM model. The model takes fiscal policy GGG and TTT, monetary policy MMM, and the price level PPP as exogenous. Given these exogenous variables, the IS curve provides the combinations of rrr and YYY that satisfy the equation representing the goods market, and the LM curve provides the combinations of rrr and YYY that satisfy the equation representing the money market.

The equilibrium of the economy is the point at which the IS curve and the LM curve cross. This point gives the interest rate rrr and the level of income YYY that satisfy conditions for equilibrium in both the goods market and the money market. In other words, at this intersection, actual expenditure equals planned expenditure, and the demand for real money balances equals the supply.
